Accredited Introduction to Phlebotomy Course (Stage 1) | Â£247 per person

Our comprehensive 2-day introductory course combines teaching of essential phlebotomy theory online and classroom teaching of practical blood taking skills. Our expert trainers will lead interactive presentations covering the requisite technical and scientific background, after which you will spend a day developing practical phlebotomy skills on life-size mannequins and vein pads.

The course has no formal entry requirements, though participants must have a working level of English. On completion, you will receive a Level 3 Certificate in Introduction to Phlebotomy (FP139) from the national awarding body, OCN Credit4Learning. You will also attain 2 credits to contribute to related qualifications you may be pursuing.

You will also be ready to progress to our Live Bloods Clinic Session (Stage 2) which take place in an NHS facility and award an NHS Certificate of Competency on completion.

Phlebotomy Live Bloods Clinic Sessions (Stage 2) | Â£290 per person

Taking place in a busy medical practice, the second stage of our training gives you the opportunity to take live bloods from real patients under the 1-to-1 supervision of an experienced phlebotomist. These sessions will allow you to apply and further develop your technical and interpersonal skills in an authentic clinical setting, performing the procedures you will regularly carry out as a practising phlebotomist.

Your performance will be assessed against the competencies set out in the Skills for Health CHS132 National Occupational Standard for Obtaining Venous Blood Samples. Once your knowledge and skills meet the necessary requirements, you will receive an NHS Certificate of Competency awarded by the facility in which you undertake your sessions, concluding the training you need to begin working as a qualified practitioner.

Dates are arranged following successful completion of the Introductory Course (Stage 1), and participants must be Hepatitis B vaccinated (1st Jab only) and Covid-19 fully vaccinated.

Please note that our partner NHS clinics have a limited number of sessions available per week for our students to complete Stage 2 training. The standard wait time for Stage 2 training is a minimum of 3-4 weeks once Stage 1 training is completed. Our partner NHS clinics may ask you to change the booking dates or cancel the session at short notice. We apologise for any inconvenience.
